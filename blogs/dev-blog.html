Web Development Journey

September 15, 2022

Skip if you don't like intros

From College Life to Web Development Bootcamp: My Journey to Becoming a Web Developer. My four years of college life as a computer science student were some of the best years of my life. I enjoyed every bit of it, from staying up late to cram for final exams to partying with my friends on weekends. However, as graduation approached, I began to worry about my future. What would I do after college? What kind of job would I find? Would I enjoy it as much as college?

Let's talk about how it started

After much research and contemplation, I decided to pursue a career in web development. I was fascinated by the endless possibilities of building websites and web applications for businesses and individuals. I knew that I had the basic coding knowledge required for web development from my computer science courses, but I needed to get more hands-on experience and learn more advanced concepts to become a professional.

That's when I discovered web development bootcamps. These intensive, short-term programs promised to equip me with the skills and knowledge I needed to break into the industry. I applied and got accepted into one of the best bootcamps in the city.

The bootcamp was a challenging yet exciting experience. I was assigned to daily coding assignments, group projects, and personal challenges. I had to learn a lot in a short amount of time. The curriculum covered various programming languages and frameworks such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Node.js, and more. I spent long hours every day perfecting my coding skills, debugging my mistakes, and collaborating with other students.

By the end of the bootcamp, I felt confident in my abilities to build web applications from scratch. I had learned how to structure HTML pages, style them using CSS, and make them dynamic using JavaScript. I had learned how to use React, a popular JavaScript library for developing user interfaces, and Node.js, a runtime environment for executing JavaScript on the server-side. I had also learned how to store data in databases, deploy applications and manage projects effectively.

Now as a web developer, I use the skills I learned in college and in the bootcamp on a daily basis. I work with clients to design and develop websites and web-apps that are visually attractive, user-friendly and efficient. I continue to learn and explore new technologies and frameworks to stay updated with the latest industry trends. I enjoy the flexibility, creativity, and challenges that come with being a web developer.
